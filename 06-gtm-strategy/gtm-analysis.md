# GTM Strategy & Client Acquisition

## Market Opportunity

### India NBFC landscape
- **10,000+** RBI-registered NBFCs as of 2024
- **~1,400** NBFC-MFIs (Microfinance Institutions) — strong FiNSTA fit
- **~100,000+** Nidhi companies registered under MCA
- **~100,000+** PACS (Primary Agricultural Credit Societies)
- **~1,600** Urban Cooperative Banks
- Total addressable market (TAM): organisations legally required to upgrade to RBI Digital Lending 2022 compliance — this is a mandatory technology upgrade cycle, not optional adoption

### The regulatory tailwind
RBI Digital Lending Guidelines 2022 created a **forced upgrade cycle** for every digital lending platform in India. NBFC clients of competitors using non-compliant software must migrate or upgrade. FiNSTA, positioned as "RBI Digital Lending 2022 compliant out-of-box," has a direct acquisition window during 2025–2026.

### FiNSTA's differentiators (to develop and market)
1. **AA-framework ready (target Q2):** Consent-based financial data access for credit assessment — few competitors offer this for the Nidhi/MACS segment
2. **RBI Digital Lending 2022 compliant out-of-box (target Q2):** Full KFS, LSP disclosure, cooling-off enforcement — market as a compliance guarantee, not a feature
3. **Kapil Group captive proof-point:** Live deployment at Kapil Chits — real NBFC operational reference
4. **Andhra Pradesh / Telangana chit fund belt:** Deep domain knowledge in the largest chit fund geography in India
5. **Offline-first mobile (target Q3):** Loan origination without internet — critical differentiator for rural NBFC field agents

## SaaS Pricing Transition

### Current model

### Problems with perpetual licence model
- Caps lifetime revenue per client (one-time sale)
- High implementation cost blocks SME NBFCs from adopting
- No natural expansion revenue (no seat-based or usage-based growth)
- Difficult to fund continuous product development

### Proposed SaaS subscription tiers

| Tier | Target segment | Pricing basis | Suggested range |
|------|---------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Starter** | Nidhi companies, small MACS (<500 members) | Per active member/month | ₹10–20 per member/month |
| **Growth** | Mid-NBFCs, PACS, cooperative banks (500–5,000 borrowers) | Per active loan account/month | ₹50–100 per account/month |
| **Enterprise** | Large NBFCs, RRBs (5,000+ borrowers) | Flat annual subscription + implementation | ₹15–40 lakh/year |
| **Kapil Group internal** | Group entities | Internal transfer pricing | Cost + 15% margin |

> **Note:** Final pricing must be validated against current deal sizes (from CRM) and competitive benchmarking. Adjust tiers based on discovered ARR data.

### Transition plan
- All new contracts from Month 6: subscription only
- Existing clients: offer migration incentive (1 year at same cost as current licence)
- Implementation services remain separate (time & materials) — SaaS fee covers product only
